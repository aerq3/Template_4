Popular Dishes in Egypt

Mahshi is a traditional dish in many Arab cuisines made from vegetables such as zucchini, eggplant, and peppers, stuffed with rice, meat, and spices, and cooked in tomato sauce until tender.

Rice pudding is a popular dessert in many cultures, made from rice cooked in milk with sugar, often flavored with ingredients like cinnamon or vanilla and topped with nuts or coconut.

Vegetable salad is a healthy and nutritious dish made from a variety of fresh vegetables such as tomatoes, cucumbers, and carrots, often served with different dressings like olive oil and vinegar.

FAMOUS EYGPTION DESSERTS

Kunafa

Mon. May 25th 2020

A dessert made from thin strands of knafeh, typically filled with akawi cheese or cream, then baked and soaked in syrup. Preheat the oven to 180°C (350°F). Rub the knafeh strands with melted butter until well-coated. Place half of the knafeh in a greased pan, then add the cheese or cream on top. Cover the filling with the remaining knafeh and press gently. Bake for 30-40 minutes until golden brown. Finally, remove from the oven and pour hot syrup over it, garnishing with crushed pistachios.

Baklava

Mon. May 25th 2020

A rich dessert made of thin layers of phyllo dough filled with nuts, soaked in syrup. Preheat the oven to 180°C (350°F). Grease a baking dish with melted butter. Place a layer of phyllo dough in the dish and brush with butter. Repeat this process with several layers of phyllo. Sprinkle ground nuts mixed with cinnamon on top. Add more layers of phyllo on top and cut into squares. Bake for 30-40 minutes until golden. Remove from the oven and fill it with hot syrup.

Qatayef

Mon. May 25th 2020

Pancakes stuffed with cream or nuts, either fried or baked and soaked in syrup. Mix flour, yeast, and sugar in a bowl. Gradually add water and mix until you have a smooth batter. Heat a pan over medium heat, then pour a portion of the batter to form a circle. Cook until bubbles appear on the surface, then let it cool. Stuff the pancakes with cream or nuts, fold them, and fry in oil until golden. Finally, soak them in hot syrup and serve.

Semidiyya

Mon. May 25th 2020

A light dessert made from semolina and sugar, cooked until golden brown. In a pot, heat the oil and then add the semolina, stirring until golden. In another pot, mix sugar, water, and cardamom, and bring to a boil. Add the hot syrup to the semolina while stirring continuously. Let it cook until it thickens, then pour into a dish and cut into squares. Serve with syrup.
